Coastal Contracts' 3 mths ended June 2016 net profit down 63%

KUALA LUMPUR - Coastal Contracts Bhd's net profit for the three months ended June 30, 2016 fell 63% to RM12.87 million or 2.43 sen per share from RM34.83 million or 6.56 sen per share in the same period a year ago, due to weaker offshore support vessels (OSV) newbuild pricing.


In a statement today, Coastal Contracts, which had changed its financial year end from Dec 31 to June 30, reported a revenue of RM200.84 million for the three months ended June 30, 2016, 22.4% higher compared to the revenue of RM164.01 million for the three-month period ended June 30, 2015.


For the 18-month period ended June 30, 2016, the group's net profit came in at RM167.05 million or 31.47 sen per share, 11.5% lower compared to a net profit of RM188.71 million or 36.26 sen per share for the 12-month period ended Dec 31, 2014, due to weaker pricing for OSV newbuilds in line with the dampened OSV market. Revenue for the 18-month period came in at RM2 billion, compared to RM877.21 million for the 12-month period ended Dec 31, 2014, mainly attributable to the sale of a jack-up rig, Coastal Driller 4001.


The group is also optimistic of making its entry into the liquefied natural gas (LNG) market in Indonesia, following the expected conclusion of the purchase of a 49% stake in PT Jaya Samudra Karunia Gas (JSK Gas) in the fourth quarter of 2016 for approximately US$20.8 million (RM84.1 million). Coastal Contracts had on July 30, 2016 entered into a memorandum of understanding with JSK Gas and various vendors to formalise the acquisition.


JSK Gas, through its 99%-owned subsidiary PT Benoa Gas Terminal (BGT), would be engaged in the operations and transfer of a floating LNG regasification unit (FRU) over a five-year contract period. Also, JSK Gas, through BGT's 99%-owned subsidiary PT Jaya Transportasi LNG, has a 10-year charter contract with the same counterparty to build and operate a floating LNG storage unit (FSU), with optional extension of another 13 years.


The FSU would receive and store LNG before it is transferred to the FRU for regasification. Coastal Contracts executive chairman Ng Chin Heng said the group is on track to complete the transaction in the fourth quarter of this year. Coastal Contracts shares closed down 3 sen (2%) to RM1.46 today, for a market capitalisation of RM774.62 million.
